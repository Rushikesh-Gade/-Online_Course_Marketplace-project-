# Faculty Questions — Online Course Marketplace

---

**Question 1: Explain in at least 100 words the reasons, why should the department approve your project?**

This project covers full-stack web development, database design, real-time communication, payment integration, API usage, and cloud-based storage — all core to the IT curriculum. It goes beyond a basic CRUD system by handling complex workflows like video delivery, role-based access, revenue sharing, and certificate generation. The two additional features — Virtual Study Room and AI Study Assistant — demonstrate initiative and the ability to integrate modern technologies like WebSockets and generative AI. The platform has real-world practical value and can be used by actual students and instructors, making it an industry-relevant final year submission that reflects both depth of knowledge and personal initiative.

---

**Question 2: What are the strengths and limitations of your proposed work?**

**Strengths:**
- Covers the complete learning lifecycle in a single platform
- Two unique features (Virtual Study Room and AI Study Assistant) differentiate it from existing platforms
- Built on free, open-source, industry-standard technologies
- Mobile responsive and accessible on all devices
- Scalable REST API architecture with role-based access control

**Limitations:**
- No native mobile app (responsive web only in current version)
- AI Study Assistant depends on an external API (Google Gemini) and requires internet access
- Video storage is limited by Cloudinary's free tier quota
- Live video conferencing for live classes is planned as future work
- Not yet deployed on a production cloud server with auto-scaling

---

**Question 3: Explain in at least 100 words what will be the positive impact of the project on society/academics/college/industry.**

This platform makes quality online education accessible to students in rural and semi-urban areas who cannot afford costly coaching or travel to institutions. The Virtual Study Room reduces the isolation commonly felt in online learning by enabling real-time peer collaboration. The AI Study Assistant allows students to learn independently without always needing a teacher present, making education more self-sufficient. For instructors, the earnings and payout system creates a new income opportunity, encouraging skilled professionals to share knowledge. From an academic perspective, the project demonstrates practical application of cloud computing, AI integration, and real-time web technologies — skills directly valued in today's industry, benefiting the student, the college, and future employers.
